Our goal is to raise $500,000. However, investors may purchase as many units as they desire (up to 20 total units) at the price of $25,000 per unit. Each unit represents 2.5% company profits, reaching a maximum of 50% company profits if utilizing all 20 units. Our minimum raise is $100,000, meaning we will not spend money on development until at least 4 units ($100,000) is raised.

Percent stake in company is not the only way development investors will see a return for their investment. Once the movie goes into production, development investors will also see an automatic return of the full amount they put in, plus 20%, which will be paid to them using the production budget. So basically, as long as the movie is made development investors will see a return on their investment, and everything from box office returns, to DVD income, given to the company, is simply extra profit. See below for more information.

YOUR RETURN ON INVESTMENT (ROI)

The Company is confident that it will create a motion picture that audiences will enjoy, thereby reaping the financial rewards from that success. The Company proposes to financially reward development investors in the following manner.

(i) At the beginning of principal photography, in addition to fully funding the production, the picture’s budget will pay back all development investors the full amount they put in plus 20%.
(ii) Once the company begins to generate revenue, the Company will use the “Producer’s Gross Income” to first pay for any and all outstanding production expenses not covered by the production budget, such as deferred pay of talent and 'above the line' salaries, distributor's fee, and other such costs. The company will then pay Production Investors (those whose investments pay for the budget of the movie) their due share. (This amounts typically to 40-60%). What’s left over is called “Company Gross Income”.

(Note: “Producer’s Gross Income” is defined as the net sum of money due to CURE OF ARS, LLC after the distribution company has deducted their fees and all costs incurred in the distribution and marketing of the film from gross revenues. ”Company Gross Income” is the company’s profit after all owed payments to 3rd parties, including those to Production Investors. )

(iii) All future Company’s Gross Income will be disbursed to development investors and filmmakers according to unit-holdings in CURE OF ARS, LLC, applied equally based on percentages of ownership. Accounting statements from distribution companies are usually handled on a quarterly basis for the first two years of the distribution agreement and semiannually thereafter for a period of two years. Accounting statements are then handled on an individual basis. Generally, the distribution company is responsible for providing statements and paying any sums due to the company within sixty days of the end of each quarter. The Company will provide the investors with accounting statements and payments due and owing, if any, within, sixty days of the receipt of those statements and moneys from the distributor.

In the event the movie is not produced within 24-36 months, all development investors will be paid back (minus sunk costs). Development investors may, at this time, also be given the opportunity to continue to be invested if producers believe they are close to getting the movie made.

Simplified example of a 12-step schedule for Development Investors:

Step 1: Development Investors place money in dedicated Cure of Ars, LLC development account.

Step 2: Development goes active. Producers package “elements” (including stars, directors, etc.) and seek to acquire guaranteed distribution, studio production financing and/or independent production financing.

Step 3: Producers negotiate distribution fees with distributors, and percent ownership in CURE OF ARS, LLC with studio production financiers and/or independent production investors.

Step 4: Filmmakers begin pre-production on film.

Step 5: At beginning of principal photography, development investors are paid back in full plus 20%.

Step 6: Filmmakers begin production on film. Production costs are covered by production investors and/or studio financiers

Step 7: Filmmakers finish production and begin post-production and distribution marketing on film. Distribution and marketing costs are covered by the distributor.

Step 8: Theatrical distribution begins. Revenue is generated. Theatrical fees and prints & advertising costs (“P&A”) provided by the distributor are some of the first to be paid back.

Step 9: After the distributor’s marketing costs are covered, the distributor takes their negotiated cut. The rest is given to CURE OF ARS, LLC.

Step 10: Before distributing profits, CURE OF ARS, LLC will first pay outstanding production expenses, including deferred guild fees, “above-the-line” fees, etc.

Step 11: CURE OF ARS, LLC will then pay studio and/or independent production investors their share. These are the investors whose investments finance the budget of the movie. Percentage ownership of production investors vary, but usually range between 40% to 60%.

Step 12: All future Company’s Gross Income will be dispersed to development investors and filmmakers according to unit-holdings in CURE OF ARS, LLC, applied equally based on percentages of ownership.
